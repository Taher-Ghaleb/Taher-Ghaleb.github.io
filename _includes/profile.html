I am currently a Ph.D. student at Queen's University at Kingston, Ontario, Canada. I obtained my Master's degree in Computer Science at KFUPM, Saudi Arabia in January 2016. My Master's thesis was on a topic related to program analysis, reverse engineering, and program visualization to enhance program comprehension. I did my Bachelors in Information Technology at Taiz University, Taiz, Yemen (September 2003 - July 2008). My BS graduation project was related to telephony applications and IVR systems. After earning the bachelor's degree, I worked at Taiz University as a full-time Teaching Assistant for three years (December 2008 - December 2011).

My domain experience is on XML databases, program analysis, reverse engineering, program comprehension, programming languages, aspect-oriented programming, and extensible compilers.

My current research interests include software re-engineering and mining software repositories.
